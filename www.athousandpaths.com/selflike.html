What is Self?

In IFS, Self is your innate core consciousness.
Self isn’t a part—it’s the essence of you.

It has the 8 C qualities:

  • Calm

  • Curiosity

  • Clarity

  • Compassion

  • Confidence

  • Courage

  • Creativity

  • Connectedness

When you’re leading from Self, you feel:

  • spacious

  • grounded

  • present

  • non-reactive

  • open-hearted

Self doesn’t have an agenda other than healing, connection, and truth.

What are Self-Like Parts?

A Self-like Part is a manager part that imitates Self energy—but isn’t Self.

It often sounds:

  • spiritual

  • wise

  • calm

  • neutral

  • “above it all”

  • reassuring

But beneath the surface, it still has:

  • a protective agenda

  • fear

  • pressure

  • tension

  • a desire to control or manage the system

These parts often learned to act like Self because that persona kept the system safe.

How to Tell the Difference

Self feels spacious; Self-like parts feel tight or “performing.”

Self:

“I’m here with you.”
This feels open.

Self-like part:

“Okay, let’s stay calm.”
This often feels effortful.

Self is curious; Self-like parts are invested in an outcome.

Self:

“I wonder what this part needs.”

Self-like part:

“Let’s get this healed, fixed, or under control.”

Self doesn’t rush; Self-like parts subtly pressure.

Self:

“We can take as long as needed.”

Self-like:

“Let’s keep moving so we can get the work done.”

Self welcomes all parts; Self-like parts are judgey, and have an intention of ‘achieving’ something.

Self:

“I can be with your anger.”

Self-like:

“Let’s stay positive.”
(or)
“Let’s not go there right now.”

Self has compassion; Self-like parts perform compassion.

A Self-like part can speak in a very spiritual or enlightened tone,
but something feels slightly hollow or performative.

Why We Have Self-Like Parts

Many people—especially those with trauma, spiritual training, or strong manager systems—develop parts that learned:

  • “If I act calm, no one will hurt me.”

  • “If I seem enlightened, people will trust me.”

  • “If I stay in control, emotions won't overwhelm the system.”

Self-like parts often emerge from:

  • childhood fawning or appeasing

  • spiritual bypassing

  • perfectionism

  • hyper-independence

  • therapeutic over-efforting

They’re usually trying to help by imitating what they believe Self should look like.

How to Work With Self-Like Parts

The key is not to fight or suppress them.

Instead:

  1. Notice the “self-like” signature (pressure, tightness, agenda).

  2. Turn toward that part with compassion.

  3. Ask:

    • “What are you afraid would happen if you didn’t manage the system this way?”

  4. Let it share its fears.

  5. Appreciate its efforts.

  6. Invite it to step back and let actual Self lead.

When this happens, the real Self energy expands.

Why It Matters

Recognizing the difference allows:

  • deeper unblending

  • more authentic Self-leadership

  • healing that isn’t performative or spiritualized

  • faster access to exiles

  • protectors to trust the system more

Many people experience a huge shift when they realize:

“Oh! That ‘wise voice’ wasn’t Self… it was a manager trying to keep things safe.”

This realization opens the door to true Self-energy.

Class Safety Agreement

Clarity about our purpose: 

To deepen our capacity to do IFS and to develop our Self-leadership as human beings.

Choice: 
You are free to censor what you disclose. No is a complete sentence. Choose to share ONLY what you feel comfortable with.

Confidentiality: 
All material everyone shares should not be shared with people who are not members of this group, even if you don’t use their name. You’d be surprised how far stories can travel. To read more, visit https://www.athousandpaths.com/safety-story.

Speak up for Safety: 
If you are having difficulty with something that’s triggered you, PLEASE speak for your parts, either directly in the group or as soon as you can afterwards. Please LISTEN to your parts when they tell you what they need in order to feel safe. Safety is our number one value. When you speak for your safety needs, you speak for the safety needs of the whole group. 

Speaking for parts, not from parts: 
To the extent that you can, speaking for parts, not from them, will help us maintain safety when sharing in the group. 

Embodying parts: 
During coaching demos, in distinction to other times during the group, feel free to speak FROM parts and to experiment with embodying them. The difference between blending and embodying is: when you are blended, you are in a part and you don’t know you are in a part. When you embody a part, you are in a part and YOU and the part BOTH know you are ‘being’ the part. 

No Coercion: 
"Thank you, for letting me, be myself!” In this group, there is “no crosstalk.” This means when one person shares, no one judges, gives advice, or reacts against what they shared.

Feedback:  Share how you are inspired or how you resonate with others. DO NOT share judgement, advice, or criticism. If you criticize others in the class, I reserve the right to determine that you may not be the right fit for my classes. Thank you for understanding. 


https://radicalifs.substack.com offers endless examples of how parts crosstalk and then repair, if you’re interested in a deeper dive. 


Timeliness: 
We commit to start and end on time. We start on time; this increases safety and strengthens the container. 

Support and responsibility: 
Ultimately, the only person who can keep you fully safe in a group is you. I want to do everything I can to make this group a safe space to learn, to go inside, and to share, in whatever ways feel right for you — but you are responsible for listening to the sometimes difficult-to-hear voices who tell you what they REALLY need to be safe. 


You are not crazy if you get triggered, it’s ULTRA NORMAL in IFS work. So take care of yourself, and possibly give yourself extra support on days when you do IFS!

Thank you for your commitment to this safety agreement!
